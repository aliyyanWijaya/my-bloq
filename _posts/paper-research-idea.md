The university offers an internship program to bridge the gap between academic teaching and industry requirements. This program ensures the curriculum remains aligned with the needs of the modern workforce.

The government initiated the program ten years ago after identifying a gap between job vacancies and job-ready workers. In response, the authorities collaborated with universities to develop a program addressing this issue.

Over the course of two years, students develop distinctive qualities such as a commitment to lifelong learning, the ability to contribute meaningfully to society, and leadership skills. They also cultivate professional integrity, the capacity to work both independently and collaboratively, and the ability to conduct critical evaluations.
